Bankruptcy Filing Checklist for St. Louis

Use this checklist to prepare for your bankruptcy filing. Being organized speeds up the process and reduces costs.

Documents to Gather

  • Federal and state tax returns (last 2 years)
  • Pay stubs or proof of income (last 6 months)
  • Bank statements (last 6 months, all accounts)
  • Most recent mortgage statement
  • Vehicle loan statements and registration
  • Credit card statements (all accounts)
  • Medical bills
  • Student loan statements
  • Lawsuit papers or garnishment notices
  • Insurance policies
  • Retirement account statements (401k, IRA)
  • Divorce decree or separation agreement (if applicable)
  • Business financial records (if self-employed)

Steps Before Filing

  1. Complete credit counseling - Required within 180 days before filing. Approved agencies at justice.gov.
  2. Stop using credit cards - Charges within 90 days of filing may not be dischargeable
  3. Do not transfer property - Transfers within 2 years can be reversed by the trustee
  4. Do not repay family members - Payments to insiders within 1 year are recoverable preferences
  5. File all tax returns - You must be current on tax filings
  6. Pull your credit report - Ensure all debts are listed in your petition

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Running up credit card debt before filing
  • Transferring property to family members
  • Paying back favored creditors (family, friends)
  • Hiding assets or income
  • Taking on new loans
  • Not listing all creditors in the petition

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to prepare?

Most people need 1-2 weeks to gather all documents. Your attorney may need an additional 1-2 weeks to prepare the petition.

Can I file without all documents?

You can file an emergency petition (bare-bones), but you must provide remaining documents within 14 days. Missing the deadline can result in dismissal.
